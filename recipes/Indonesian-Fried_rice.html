Indonesian Fried rice

Indonesian Fried rice. "My Favorite easy-to-make Food Choice"

When you have leftover rice. It's easy to make it.
Easy to cook, easy on Ingredients, and easy to serve.

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon shrimp paste (terasi), optional
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sliced cucumber and tomato for garnish
  • Fried shallots for topping
  • 2 cups cooked cold rice (preferably leftover rice)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 red chili peppers, thinly sliced (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce

Recipe instructions

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large frying pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the minced garlic, chopped onion, and sliced chili peppers.
  3. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes until fragrant and the onion becomes translucent.
  4. Push the garlic, onion, and chili mixture to one side of the pan, creating a space in the middle.
  5. Pour the beaten eggs into the space and scramble them until they are cooked through.
  6. Add the cold cooked rice to the pan and break up any clumps with a spatula or fork.
  7. Stir-fry the rice together with the garlic, onion, chili, and eggs for a few minutes.
  8. In a small bowl, mix together the sweet soy sauce, soy sauce, and shrimp paste (if using).
  9. Pour the sauce mixture over the rice and stir-fry until the rice is evenly coated and heated through.
  10. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust the seasoning and soy sauce according to your preference.
  11. Remove from heat and garnish with sliced cucumber, tomato, and fried shallots.
  12. Serve the Indonesian fried rice hot as a main dish or with other accompaniments such as fried egg, chicken satay, or prawn crackers.
